# D³ — Constitution

> **The artifact is not `constitution.json`. The artifact is the question the developer didn't know to ask.**

Doctrine-Driven Development. This document answers only one thing: *what kind of system is this?*
Not how. Not yet.

- **Evidence before assertion.** Never claim more than you can prove today.
- **The first artifact is a constitution, not a backlog.**
- **Decisions are deferred until evidence deserves them.** Intelligently postponing the decisions that
  do not yet merit becoming architecture is the point — not a side effect.
- **The law is the compiled, immutable contract.** Memory and conversation preserve lessons; they are
  not law.
- **One authority per decision.**
- **Discovery opens relevant uncertainty; it never closes it falsely.** A Discovery that produces only
  answers is fake.
- **An ADR is not an initial decision — it is the crystallization of what a slice proved.**
- **The methodology applies to itself.** If a claim cannot be refuted, it is not doctrine.
- **Stack-agnostic.** The discipline already survived three languages and domains (Milpa/PHP,
  OverlayKit/TypeScript, DOM Protocol). It is not caged in one.

**Thesis:** *Good software starts with better questions. Doctrine turns those questions into architecture.*
