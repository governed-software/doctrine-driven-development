# Discovery — the questions worth answering (D³ on D³)

Not solutions. Questions.

1. What problem does D³ solve, for whom, and why does that problem exist?
2. What happens to a project if D³ does nothing? (If nothing bad happens, D³ is unnecessary.)
3. What is the smallest evidence that a guided Discovery is worth the developer's first hour?
4. How does a discipline become the *path of least resistance* instead of a tax? (H-001 is one bet —
   is it the only one?)
5. What makes a question *architectural* rather than merely interesting?
   (Working answer: when its answer changes the order of construction.)
6. Can one Discovery serve a new project and an existing one — or is retrofit a different animal?
   (Evidence so far: different animal.)
7. Who is the authority for a project's constitution when the team is one person?
   (Prior art: OverlayKit's honestly-declared `bootstrap-authority`.)
8. What does D³ **refuse** to do, so it never becomes the heavy methodology it is trying to replace?
